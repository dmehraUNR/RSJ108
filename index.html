How to Cultivate a Source

Time Needed: 2 hours
Effectiveness rating: 100% effective

Do you struggle with finding human sources for your journalistic work? Below are some steps I follow to meet people with relevant knowledge, befriend and create a personal connection with them, and then use their connections and information to increase the quality of my articles and other works.

  • Requirements

  • An outgoing attitude
  • Willingness to cooperate
  • Some Determination
  • Honesty

  1. Socialize and Be Friendly!

    Find a common ground and look to make friends before you think of anyone as a source. If you go into every social interaction looking to gain something out of it, you won't make a personal connection with anyone.

  2. Network!

    The saying ‘It’s not about what you know, but who you know’ cannot be more true in any profession than journalism. Networking is very important because sometimes a person you already know will have a connection with somebody that may have relevant information for you regarding the topic of your article or project.

    You'll never know who you will meet!

  3. Gain Trust!

    The only way another individual would be willing to exchange information with you is if they trust you and believe that what you are doing is in good faith. There are many ways to do this such as exchanging information or sharing proprietary information. It shows you are

  4. Be Genuine and Honest!

    The last thing a journalist wants to do is come off disingenuous or hide their true intentions under false pretenses. Be open with what your motives are. If the person you are trying to gain information from is not willing to take part, let them make that decision under their own will.

  5. Pay it Forward!

    Be sure to be reasonable and accessible to other people as well. Just like you may have reached out to a friend earlier, someone may reach out to you regarding information for whatever they are working on. Be helpful, as you never know when you will need access to their knowledge about a particular subject.
